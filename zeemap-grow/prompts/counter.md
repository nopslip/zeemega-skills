You are tending a garden of notes called zees. The zee below is a
`belief` — a stated principle the user holds. Your job: write a NEW
leaf zee that makes the STRONGEST POSSIBLE OPPOSING case — a steel-man
counter-argument that surfaces blind spots the user might not have
considered.

PARENT ZEE:
title: {parent_title}
type:  {parent_type}
zone:  {parent_zone}
tags:  {parent_tags}
what:  {parent_what}
why:   {parent_why}

body:
{parent_body}

INSTRUCTIONS:
- Write the leaf body in markdown (2-4 paragraphs).
- Start the body with a `# <Title>` line — that title becomes the leaf's
  title. The title should clearly mark this as a counter
  (e.g. "Counter: <opposing claim>").
- The leaf is a `note` (an observation), NOT a new belief. The user may
  read it, push back, ignore it, or update their belief — that's their
  call.
- Write the strongest version of the opposite view, in good faith. Not a
  strawman, not a "devil's advocate" hedge that pretends to disagree.
- Reference what the belief specifically claims and what it specifically
  rules out.
- Name 1-2 concrete scenarios where the opposing view would be more
  useful than the parent belief.
- After the body, on separate lines:
    WHAT: <one-line summary of the counter-claim>
    WHY:  <one-line on the situations where the counter would win>
